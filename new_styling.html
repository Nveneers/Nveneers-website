What Are N Veneers?

A Revolution in
Cosmetic Dentistry

Dental N Veneers are a patented brand of ultra-thin porcelain veneers — roughly 0.2 mm thick, about the width of a contact lens — that are bonded directly over your natural teeth. Crafted from proprietary Cerinate porcelain, they require little to no enamel removal, making them a minimally invasive and often reversible procedure.

Step by Step

The N Veneer Procedure

01

Consultation & Assessment

Your dentist evaluates your oral health, discusses your aesthetic goals, and confirms that N Veneers are the right solution for your unique case. Any underlying issues such as decay or gum disease are addressed before proceeding.

02

Impressions & Design

Detailed impressions or digital scans of your teeth are taken and sent to a certified Cerinate laboratory. Working with your dentist, you select the ideal shape, size, and shade for your new smile. Fabrication typically takes 2–4 weeks.

03

Fitting & Bonding

Once your custom N Veneers arrive, your dentist checks the fit and color, makes any final adjustments, and bonds them to your teeth with a strong dental adhesive. No anesthesia is typically required. You leave with your new smile the same day.

04

Follow-Up

A brief check-up ensures your N Veneers are properly seated and that your bite feels comfortable. Most patients adapt quickly with no significant adjustment period.

Balanced Overview

Advantages & Disadvantages

Advantages

  • Minimally invasive — no drilling or injections required in most cases
  • Reversible, as natural tooth structure is preserved
  • Long-lasting — up to 20 years with proper care
  • Highly stain-resistant porcelain material
  • Corrects chips, gaps, discoloration, and worn teeth in just two visits
  • Ideal for patients with dental anxiety
  • No temporary veneers needed during fabrication

Disadvantages

  • Can appear slightly bulkier, especially on larger teeth
  • Not suited for severe misalignment or structural damage
  • May not fully mask very dark or heavily stained teeth
  • Not covered by dental insurance (cosmetic procedure)
  • Not recommended for patients with bruxism (teeth grinding)
  • Some patients notice a brief adjustment period with their bite
Is It Right for You?

Ideal Candidates

N Veneers work best for adults in good oral health who want to address specific cosmetic concerns without invasive treatment. You may be an ideal candidate if you have one or more of the following:

🦷

Tooth Discoloration

Staining that does not respond to professional whitening treatments.

✦

Chips & Cracks

Minor chips or surface cracks that affect the appearance of your smile.

◦

Small Gaps

Spaces between teeth that you wish to close without orthodontic treatment.

⟡

Misshapen Teeth

Teeth that are uneven, irregular, or slightly out of proportion.

⌛

Worn Enamel

Teeth that appear shorter or worn down due to natural aging.

🛡

Dental Anxiety

Patients who prefer a painless, no-drill alternative to conventional veneers.

Long-Term Care

Aftercare & Maintenance

Caring for your N Veneers is simple. With the right habits, your investment can last up to two decades.

🪥

Brush Twice Daily

Use a soft-bristled toothbrush and a non-abrasive toothpaste to protect the porcelain surface.

🦺

Wear a Night Guard

If you grind your teeth at night, a custom guard protects your N Veneers from excessive force.

🗓

Regular Check-Ups

Visit your dentist every six months for professional cleaning and to monitor the condition of your veneers.

🧊

Avoid Hard Foods

Do not bite directly into hard items like ice, hard candy, or bones with your veneered teeth.
